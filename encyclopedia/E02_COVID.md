## SECTION 4 — TRANSMISSION MECHANISM
*Full causal chain from macro trigger to deal-level impact.*
*Every link states the mechanism, the asset class, and the timeline.*

**Primary chain — risk (revenue-risk infrastructure):**
Pandemic declaration → global lockdowns → human movement collapse → toll road traffic -70 to -95% → airport passenger volumes -90%+ → port throughput -40 to -60% → revenue-risk P3 cash flows collapse → DSCR covenant breaches within 60-90 days → lenders activate covenant waiver process → government support packages negotiated → recovery U-shaped over 24-36 months as mobility restored.
Timeline to capital stack impact: 30-60 days (revenue). Timeline to covenant breach: 60-90 days.

**Primary chain — validation (availability-payment infrastructure):**
Pandemic declaration → lockdowns → government counterparty obligation unchanged → availability payments continue regardless of usage → zero revenue impact on availability-payment P3s → lenders experience no impairment → structural protection empirically confirmed at scale for first time.
Timeline: immediate — first monthly availability payment post-lockdown confirmed the mechanism.

**Policy response chain — zero-rate tailwind:**
Fed emergency cuts to zero March 15 → CARES Act $2.2T March 27 → infrastructure as essential spending → zero rate environment → infrastructure debt spreads compress → 2020-21 vintage forms at L+125 to L+175 vs historical L+200 to L+250 → record low underwriting rates → record high leverage assumptions → dangerous vintage cohort created simultaneously with demand shock resolution.
Timeline: 60-90 days from Fed cuts to new deal economics resetting.

**Force majeure mechanism:**
Lockdown orders → construction sites closed by government mandate → force majeure triggered on construction contracts → EPC contractors suspend work → completion delay claims filed → construction lenders face schedule overrun without contractor fault → insurance claims process → political risk and pandemic exclusions tested → market reprices exclusions post-COVID.

**2026 relevance:**
COVID validated the availability-payment structure absolutely. Any infrastructure lender in 2026 pricing revenue-risk assets must apply the COVID stress — -70 to -95% revenue for 18 months — as the floor scenario. The 2020-21 vintage warning is live: those deals are now in years 4-6, their stress window falls 2023-2027, and the Hormuz crisis arrived at exactly that moment.

## SECTION 5 — RESOLUTION SIGNALS
*What ended the stress. First indicators of normalization.*

**What ended the stress:**
- Vaccine approvals November-December 2020 — Pfizer/BioNTech, Moderna
- Mobility data recovery — Google mobility reports showing movement returning Q3 2020
- Government support packages — aviation, toll road, port revenue support in most markets
- Fed zero rate commitment — infrastructure boom absorbed capital from risk assets
- CARES Act and ARRA-equivalent spending — construction activity resumed

**First indicators of normalization (earliest appearance):**
1. Apple and Google mobility data turning — May-June 2020 (earliest real-time signal)
2. TSA checkpoint throughput recovering — June 2020 (US aviation leading indicator)
3. Container shipping volumes recovering — Q3 2020 (port throughput following)
4. Infrastructure deal flow recovering — Q3 2020 (IJGlobal data)
5. Toll road traffic at 70% of prior year — September 2020

**Recovery timeline by asset class:**
- A23 Social infrastructure (availability-payment): no recovery needed — zero impact
- A17 Transmission and grid: no impact — regulated revenues held
- A19 Toll roads: 24-36 months to full traffic recovery (market-dependent)
- A21 Airports: 36-48 months — international aviation slowest
- A20 Ports: 18-24 months — container volumes recovered faster than passengers
- A01 Gas power / A04 Solar: minimal impact — demand for power held

**What did not resolve quickly:**
- International aviation — 2019 levels not restored until 2023-24 in most markets
- Insurance market pandemic exclusions — now standard in construction policies
- Supply chain disruption — persisted into 2022, compounding with Ukraine
- 2020-21 vintage risk — not resolved; stress window 2023-2027 still open

**Sri practitioner notes:**
The availability-payment versus revenue-risk distinction was always understood theoretically. COVID proved it empirically at a scale no stress test had ever reached. Every availability-payment P3 kept receiving its contractual payment regardless of whether a single person used the asset. Every revenue-risk P3 faced existential stress within 60 days. The force majeure mechanism on construction contracts was invoked broadly but resolved faster than expected because governments needed construction to restart — political pressure on FM resolution was significant. The dangerous legacy of COVID is not what it broke but what it created: the 2020-21 vintage formed at zero rates with leverage assumptions that have never been tested at normalized rates. That vintage is the live risk in 2026.
