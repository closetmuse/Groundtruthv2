## SECTION 4 — TRANSMISSION MECHANISM
*Full causal chain. Multi-year compound stress — longest mechanism chain in encyclopedia.*

**Primary chain — stagflation compound (all infrastructure):**
Iranian Revolution January 1979 → oil supply shock → WTI doubles → US CPI accelerates to 11% → Volcker appointed August 1979 → aggressive monetary tightening begins → Fed Funds rises from 10% to 20% → construction costs compound with energy inflation → infrastructure projects face simultaneous cost overrun and financing cost explosion → refinancing markets close → mini-perms mature with no refinancing available → projects requiring capital markets access face existential stress → abandonment or government rescue for strategic assets.
Timeline: 18-24 months from first oil shock to full compound stress. Duration: 3+ years.

**Why this is the only R1 plus R2 compound in the dataset:**
Every other stress event in the encyclopedia is either commodity shock (R3) or credit stress (R2) or geopolitical (R4) — not both R1 and R2 simultaneously. Volcker deliberately created credit stress to break commodity-driven inflation. The Fed was the second vector. This is the only time in 47 years where the cure for one stress (Volcker tightening to break inflation) created a second simultaneous stress (credit market closure). The 2026 analog: Hormuz creates commodity inflation — if FOMC responds with hikes on April 28, the same compound mechanism fires.

**The rate peak deployment thesis:**
The lenders who survived the 1979-82 period with dry powder and deployed in 1983-85 captured the best vintage in a generation. Rates were falling from 20% — every deal closed in 1983 benefited from falling WACC over its life. The deals closed in 1978-79 at trough rates faced the refinancing cliff at 20%. The Volcker lesson: in a compound R1+R2 stress, preserve capital through the peak and deploy aggressively at the turn.

**2026 compound parallel:**
Hormuz supply shock → Brent +35% → CPI pressure → FOMC April 28 constrained between inflation (hike) and growth slowdown (hold) → if hike: E08 R1+R2 compound mechanism begins → construction costs already +12.6% annualized → refinancing market already stressed via BDC gates → 2020-21 vintage mini-perm cliff arrives H2 2026 into this environment → four-vector compound with no historical precedent. The FOMC April 28 decision is the binary that determines whether 2026 resembles E07 alone or E07 plus E08.

## SECTION 5 — RESOLUTION SIGNALS
*What ended the stress. Applicable to 2026 monitoring.*

**What ended the stress:**
- Volcker pivot late 1981 — Fed signaled peak rates, began easing
- CPI breaking — inflation fell from 14.8% to under 4% by 1983
- Fed Funds falling — from 20% to 8% by end of 1983
- Oil price stabilization — Saudi Arabia increased production, OPEC discipline broke
- Refinancing markets reopening — 1983 first infrastructure refinancings

**First indicators of normalization (earliest appearance):**
1. Fed language shift — late 1981 (Volcker signaling peak)
2. UST 10Y falling from peak — Q4 1981
3. CPI monthly prints decelerating — Q3 1981
4. Oil price plateauing — 1981 before decline
5. First new infrastructure financial closes — 1983

**Recovery timeline:**
- Strategic energy infrastructure: government support through stress, recovered 1983
- Transport infrastructure: new financial closes resumed 1983-84
- Power generation: new builds resumed 1984-85 at falling rates
- The 1983-85 vintage: best performing in Moody's dataset for first decade

**What the legacy looked like in deal structures:**
Rate cap requirements became standard on mini-perms. Shorter mini-perm tenors (3 years vs 5 years) became market standard. Floating rate with cap replaced fixed rate on construction debt. Refinancing risk became a first-order underwriting consideration rather than an afterthought. These structural changes persisted for a decade and are visible in Moody's 1983-1990 vintage data.

**Sri practitioner notes:**
I was not in infrastructure finance during the Volcker period — it predates my career. But the legacy of it was visible in every deal structure I worked on in the early 2000s. The rate cap requirements, the shorter mini-perm tenors, the emphasis on refinancing risk as a primary underwriting variable — all of that came directly from the 1979-82 experience. Senior bankers in the early 2000s still talked about deals that failed not because the asset was bad but because the refinancing window happened to open when Fed Funds were at 18%. The closest modern analog I have seen is what happened to 2020-21 vintage deals facing the H2 2026 refinancing cliff — same mechanism, different magnitude. The Volcker lesson that applies directly to 2026: if the FOMC hikes on April 28 into a commodity shock, the compound stress mechanism is the same one that closed infrastructure financing markets for three years in 1980-82. The magnitude will be smaller because the Fed is not starting from 0% and targeting 20%. But the direction of the mechanism is identical.
