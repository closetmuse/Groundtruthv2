## SECTION 4 — TRANSMISSION MECHANISM
*Full causal chain from tariff trigger to infrastructure-deal-level impact.*
*Every link states the mechanism, the asset class, and the timeline.*

**Primary chain — tariff to construction cost:**
Reciprocal-tariff regime announced → import landed costs rise on tariffed goods → steel, aluminum, transformer components, cables, solar-module subassemblies repriced → US-domestic substitutes (where they exist) price up to just below import-plus-tariff ceiling → construction-material cost index rises 12-18% → EPC contract contingencies tested → cost-overrun risk on projects with procurement windows open during tariff-implementation → change-order negotiations and variation-of-scope claims.
Timeline: 0-60 days for commodity pass-through; 6-12 months for full flow-through into delivered construction cost.

**Secondary chain — cross-asset dislocation:**
Tariff announcement triggering simultaneous equity-down + bonds-down + USD-down → foreign holders of US assets rebalancing → Treasury demand weakening at the margin → fiscal-risk-premium + inflation-risk-premium priced into long duration → 30Y yield spiked 60bps in 5 days → corporate IG and HY spreads widened on uncertainty → infrastructure senior debt repriced 80-120bps wider during acute window.
Timeline: Intraday for market response; 30-90 days for primary-issuance-market repricing.

**Tertiary chain — equipment lead-time extension:**
Tariff-compound with pre-existing LPT shortage (2022-25 global LPT backlog) → domestic LPT fabricator capacity tightened further → lead times extended 18 → 30+ months → DC-anchored transmission upgrades, renewable-grid-upgrade projects, load-serving entity capex programs all face material schedule risk → projects with LPT in critical path re-sequenced or delayed.
Timeline: 12-36 months for lead-time extensions to fully flow into deal schedules.

**Cross-border EPC renegotiation mechanism:**
EPC contracts with foreign-sourced equipment commitments → tariff-stack applied to previously-priced equipment → EPC contractor seeks change order or variation → sponsor faces bid/rebid decision → delays in project financial close or completion → construction-loan extensions negotiated → senior debt maturity/refi profile shifted.
Timeline: 3-9 months from tariff implementation to renegotiated contracts.

**2026 relevance:**
Liberation Day is the first post-COVID test of tariff-as-durable-policy-instrument (not tariff-as-negotiating-bluff). The 90-day pause in April 2025 and subsequent bilateral negotiations established a rolling framework in which tariff rates are conditional on negotiated outcomes — making 6-month, 12-month tariff-rate forecasts inherently uncertain. For 2026 infrastructure: any deal with >20% import-content exposure in its capex must price tariff volatility explicitly in underwriting, not treat tariffs as point-in-time cost. BE-004 (April 8 2026 tariff deadline) resolving with "tentative ceasefire, not full pause" is typical of the post-Liberation-Day regime. Specifically for DC build-out: LPT, switchgear, advanced cooling, power-conditioning equipment are tariff-stack exposed and deal structures must reflect this. Steel and aluminum transmission is well-understood; specialty equipment transmission is still being priced into deal structures in 2026.

## SECTION 5 — RESOLUTION SIGNALS
*What ended the acute stress. First indicators of normalization.*

**What ended the acute stress:**
- 90-day reciprocal-tariff pause announced Apr 9 2025 — full reciprocal rates above 10% paused for most countries (125% kept on China)
- Bilateral negotiation frameworks established over the 90-day window
- Fed cut rates 25bps May 2025 — modest monetary cushion
- Corporate earnings Q1 2025 print — less bad than feared on tariff guidance
- Retaliatory actions from trading partners more measured than initially signaled

**First indicators of normalization (earliest appearance):**
1. VIX retracing below 30 — mid-April 2025 (one week post-pause)
2. Infrastructure primary debt issuance resuming — May 2025
3. IG spread compression to <110bps — June 2025
4. Domestic commodity premiums stabilizing (not retracing) — Q2-Q3 2025
5. Cross-asset flow patterns normalizing — USD stabilizing rather than continuing to weaken

**Recovery timeline by asset class:**
- Infrastructure senior debt: 90-180 days to recover primary-market access at normal levels (at wider but stable spreads)
- Commodity pass-through: immediate; prices remained elevated at new equilibrium post-pause
- Construction lending: 6-12 months to reprice contingencies permanently
- Import-dependent equipment (LPT, specialty): did not recover lead-times — structural shift
- Solar manufacturing build-out: 18-24 months to commission new domestic capacity

**What did not resolve quickly:**
- 10% universal baseline tariff: retained throughout 90-day pause and beyond
- Section 232 steel and aluminum tariffs: kept at 25% through the cycle
- Country-specific reciprocal rates on China and others: remain in renegotiation
- Structurally higher construction contingency budgets: permanent
- LPT and switchgear lead times: permanently extended relative to pre-tariff baseline

**Sri practitioner notes:**
Liberation Day mattered for three structural reasons beyond the acute market stress. First: it was the clearest evidence that the Trump-II administration views tariff as durable policy instrument, not negotiating threat — the post-90-day-pause bilateral-negotiation framework has been maintained, BE-004 April 8 2026 is the clearest example. Second: the cross-asset dislocation (equity-down + bonds-down + USD-down simultaneously) was the first such configuration outside a twin-deficit crisis; signaled that foreign-official demand for US assets is conditional on policy predictability, not just on rate-differential. Third: the tariff-transmission mechanism confirmed what the steel/aluminum pre-event pattern had already shown — US-domestic commodity pricing equilibrates just below the import-plus-tariff ceiling, so every 10% tariff on steel/aluminum translates to an 8-9% permanent increase in US-domestic pricing. For DC construction cost underwriting, this is now a durable feature, not a stress scenario. The 2026 compound with Hormuz (E07 ACTIVE) creates the R0 regime currently live — tariff-policy volatility on top of kinetic-crude volatility on top of credit-compression.
