Steel HRC is up 61.49% over 90 days. Copper is up 9.48% over 7 days and broke its threshold today. Aluminum is up 12.82% over 30 days. Three of the four construction inputs for utility-scale solar are running hot simultaneously, and none of them is responding to the Hormuz oil reversal because they are being driven by construction demand pull, not by fuel cost pass-through.

Project Vega is a solar-plus-storage deal with two active binary events pointing at it: OBBBA BOC deadline July 4 (T-81) and FOMC April 28-29 (T-15). OBBBA requires demonstrated beginning-of-construction by July 4 to preserve full-value ITC/PTC eligibility. BOC is measured in dollars committed and physical work performed, which means the cost base used for BOC qualification is the live cost base, not the underwritten one. A project whose BOC dollars get redirected to cover unplanned steel inflation is a project running its BOC headroom down.

The transmission mechanism is that Vega BOC economics are being eroded by the 61% steel move on the same calendar that the BOC deadline is counting down. Every week between now and July 4 that steel stays at current levels is a week of cost-base drift against a fixed-date deadline. A 200bps IRR compression from cost-stack inflation against an 81-day hard calendar is the kind of event that turns a clearly-financeable deal into a marginal one, and it does not require any external bad news to happen.

This is alpha because the OBBBA deadline conversation today is about legislative risk (will the deadline be extended) and political risk (will Treasury guidance soften). It is not about construction-cost arithmetic, which is the input that is actually moving. The political question is noisy and uncertain; the cost-stack question is observable and trending. The second question is where the platform has an informational edge.
