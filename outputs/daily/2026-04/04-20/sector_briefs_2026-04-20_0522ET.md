**Axis 4 — DOE / FERC export authorization.** **GS-VG-CP2 AMBER: "Venture Global gets pre-filing waiver for CP2 LNG expansion."** Expanded read follows.

*What the waiver is.* FERC's NGA §3 pre-filing process is a mandatory pre-application phase (typically 6–12 months) for LNG export terminals — environmental scoping, cooperating-agency coordination, stakeholder outreach, resource-report drafting. A waiver of pre-filing lets VG skip directly to a formal application on CP2 expansion capacity. This is distinct from, but procedurally upstream of, the DOE non-FTA export authorization VG also needs — a waiver here does not resolve DOE, but it removes the longest single-step procedural drag on FERC's side.

*Mechanism.* Schedule compression of ~6–12 months flows through three channels: (1) earlier COD on the incremental CP2 trains, which pulls forward VG's merchant-cargo capacity; (2) earlier EPC mobilization, which creates pull on steel/cement/labor in the Gulf Coast window that was otherwise spread further out; (3) a precedent effect — if FERC is willing to waive pre-filing for VG's second major expansion in a year, the implied agency posture on companion US LNG projects (Sabine Pass Stage 5, the broader Trump-admin-era permit queue) tightens correlation between filings and approvals. That last channel is the one that shifts the Axis 4 regulatory-kill-switch thesis.

*Timeline.* Material schedule advance is 6–12 months on CP2 expansion COD. The precedent-effect transmission to other pending filings is faster — weeks-to-months once the waiver document is analyzed by counsel at competing sponsors.

*Asset specificity.* VG CP2 directly; contracted-pipeline LNG terminals (including the two in the LNG state memo) do NOT get a direct benefit because they are already past pre-filing. The beneficiaries are expansion-phase projects and new entrants still upstream of formal application. That means VG's merchant-volume lead over contracted peers widens — the exact channel the VG merchant-risk lens was opened to track.

*What to watch for corroboration.* (a) Full text of the FERC waiver order — scope (pre-filing only vs. additional procedural accelerations) and any conditions attached. (b) DOE response on the companion non-FTA export authorization for the CP2 expansion — does the regulatory acceleration extend beyond FERC or stop at the FERC door? (c) EPC contractor commentary — Bechtel or Technip Energies acknowledging mobilization pull-forward would be the construction-cost transmission showing up. (d) Any competing-sponsor filing activity that cites the VG precedent.

*Hardening read.* Pair with the standing Sabine Pass Stage 5 Draft EIS thread from the Friday EOD capture and the overall Trump-admin LNG-permitting posture. Axis 4's HARDENING status firms: the regulatory path for US LNG expansion is visibly accelerating, not holding or tightening. This is a persistence signal on the underlying Axis 4 thesis rather than a new mechanism — but the persistence itself is information, because the Axis 4 kill-switch thesis was built on scenarios where a Democratic-era DOE moratorium returned.
