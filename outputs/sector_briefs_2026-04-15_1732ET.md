MACRO OVERLAY — MORNING READ REVERSED
================================================================

The morning consumer-buffer read is gone. At midday I flagged **GS-656 (Oil Price): "BofA Flags Gas Price Surge but No Pullback in Discretionary Spend"** as a signal that the consumer was absorbing the gas pass-through without behavioral change. By PM, the same data source has moved in the opposite direction: **GS-679 (Rigzone): "Gas Spending Jumps 16 Pct for BofA Customers Amid Iran War."** This is the same BofA credit-card data set at a more granular level — total gas spending is up 16% across BofA customer base in the window since the Iran crisis began.

Two observations:

First, the speed of the pass-through is the non-obvious part. Gas price inflation normally takes 4–8 weeks to show up in consumer discretionary prints — the consumer typically absorbs the first few weeks of price increases out of savings, then discretionary categories (restaurants, entertainment, travel) start to compress. Today's BofA print is less than six weeks into the Iran crisis (Hormuz Day 42) and gas spending is already 16% above baseline. Either the pass-through is faster than historical or the baseline was already stressed before the crisis — both readings are material.

Second, the reversal inside a single day's tape from the same data source is itself a framework-level observation. GS-656 and GS-679 are both from the same BofA customer transaction dataset, published hours apart, telling opposite stories. The likely explanation is that GS-656 was reporting a prior weekly cut of the data while GS-679 is reporting a more current weekly cut — the "no pullback" read was already stale when Oil Price ran it this morning. The framework lesson: BofA monthly-transaction prints should be treated as a leading rather than a coincident indicator, and a morning brief citing yesterday's data point can be overtaken within the same day by a more current one. I'll watch for this pattern specifically.

**Fed-independence context:** Trump-Powell escalation from the midday tape (GS-572 / GS-658 "if he stays beyond May") plus Cleveland Fed Hammack hawkish hold (GS-571) plus FT "double danger" warning (GS-596) all remain active. The cross-pressure between hawkish Fed stance and consumer-driven political demand for cuts is the thing to watch.
