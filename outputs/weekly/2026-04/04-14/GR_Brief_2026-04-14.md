Henry Hub flat at $3.04 on 7d, but **down 17.84% on 90d** — the most important LNG datapoint on tonight's board, and nothing in today's signal flow explains it.

### Deal exposure
**Project Delfin (LNG, Louisiana)** and **Project Commonwealth (LNG, Texas)** are the two named LNG-asset deals in the active pipeline. Both are Brent-slope export contracts with Henry Hub feedstock exposure — directly in the crosshairs of the 90d HH move. **GT-109 SB Energy Milam (DC, Texas/ERCOT)** hit on GS-379 (oil traders betting on diplomacy as physical supply tightens) via Texas/gas exposure, though the transmission there is secondary. **Project TotalEnergies** matched GS-236 (TotalEnergies strikes oil and gas at Moho license) — sponsor-name match, not deal-specific. No signal landed a Tier 1 match on Delfin or Commonwealth directly today.

### Non-obvious observation
**Henry Hub is down 17.84% over 90 days while Brent is up 51.67% over 90 days.** That is a 70-percentage-point spread move in 90 days between the feedstock and the Brent-linked offtake. Most US LNG project finance models are run on a static HH-to-Brent slope assumption, not on rolling 90-day realised spreads. When the spread widens this fast, the FID economics for Delfin and Commonwealth are improving faster than the capital stack is re-pricing them. The direction is correct — US LNG is a structural winner of Hormuz stress — but the magnitude is larger than consensus and the timing is earlier. Offtakers negotiating long-dated HOAs this quarter are anchoring to 30-day strips that still have the pre-crash Brent print in them; the model revision that shows the real economics is 2–4 weeks out.

### Act this week
Walk the Delfin and Commonwealth feedstock assumptions against the 90-day HH trajectory. If either operating model uses a Henry Hub curve dated pre-April, the forward-year pricing is probably 15–20% high on the cost side. This is a desk input check, not new research — a 20-minute conversation with each deal team.
