## Mechanism — why this matters for neocloud capex

Neocloud senior debt today cannot reliably reach IG because the credit stack carries physical-asset, business-interruption, power-outage, fiber-outage, and equipment-failure risks directly. Rating-agency methodology can't bridge to IG because operational-risk concentration is too high on a single-asset or single-platform basis.

ILS-wrap structure isolates those specific risk layers, places them in a casualty sidecar or operational-risk cat bond funded by third-party capital, and puts the insurer/reinsurer in first-loss position on named perils. The residual credit risk on senior debt becomes platform-obligor-plus-insurance-counterparty. If both carry IG ratings, the senior tranche has a defensible rating path to IG.

The IG threshold is binary. Crossing it opens:

- CLO demand (IG-only mandates)
- Insurance general-account balance sheets (regulatory capital treatment)
- Pension allocations (fiduciary constraints)
- Sovereign wealth fund mandates (multi-billion allocations)

The capital pool for IG DC senior paper is **10-50x the HY pool** that exists today. Applied to the $500B+ neocloud buildout trajectory, even 10% structural penetration is **$50B+ of debt capacity that does not currently exist in any accessible form**. This is why the thread is not sector color — it is a capital-structure inflection that can determine whether neocloud capex scales or bottlenecks.
